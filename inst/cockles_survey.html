In 2023 and 2024 Solway Firth cockles were surveyed by SeaScope Fisheries Research Limited and data analysed by Scottish Governmentâ€™s Marine Directorate. Nine areas that have been identified as important habitats for cockles in the Solway Firth are sampled using a random stratified sampling methodology. A two-stage adaptive survey procedure was used in survey sites with higher abundances of cockles aged two years and older.

Stations are sampled using a 0.1m2 quadrat sample. Sediment was removed to a depth of 100mm and sieved using a 5mm meshed sieve. All whole undamaged cockles collected at each sampling station are aged, measured using digital vernier callipers and weighed using a digital balance. Cockles are aged by counting external growth rings/bands. Any cockles without visible growth rings are classed as 0 group cockles (zero age group).
