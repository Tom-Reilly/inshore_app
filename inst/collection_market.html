Scotland runs two main shellfish sampling schemes—one for the mainland (including Orkney and the west coast islands) and one for Shetland.

Mainland
  • Biological data collected on five shellfish species: Nephrops (Norway lobster), scallops, lobsters, edible crabs and velvet crabs.
  • The mainland is divided into 15 sampling areas, based on geography and local landing ports and processors.
  • This scheme can potentially cover 78 % of Scotland’s shellfish landings. The remaining 22 % comes from small boats landing in remote areas.
  • Sampling is done during designated weeks, based on where and when shellfish are available. It’s not random—sampling is opportunistic.
Shetland
  • Data collection managed separately under a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU).
  • Focuses on four species (excluding Nephrops, which are not typically landed in Shetland).
  • Targets are set for how many samples to collect for each species.
Data Collection

For each shellfish, measurements are taken:

  • Shell or carapace length for all species.
  • Sex and breeding condition for Nephrops, crab and lobster.
  • Ages for scallops.

Data is used to estimate numbers by age or size, with statistical methods to calculate confidence intervals. All data is stored in national databases and analysed using a documented R package, with strict quality checks at every stage.
