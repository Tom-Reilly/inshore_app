The Marine Directorate (MD) of the Scottish Government is responsible for the management of Scotland's inshore fisheries. The MD includes several portfolios who collaborate to oversee regulation, enforcement, science and policy development within Scottish waters. Staff also work closely with stakeholders, academia and delivery partners NatureScot, Joint Nature Conservation Committee (JNCC) and the Scottish Environment Protection Agency (SEPA).

The Inshore Fisheries Management Improvement (IFMI) Programme is a recent initiative to modernize management, improve responsiveness to scientific data, and promote co-management with stakeholders.

The Regional Inshore Fisheries Groups (RIFGs) are non-statutory bodies that aim to improve the management of fisheries in the 0-12 nautical mile zone of Scottish waters and to give commercial inshore fishers a strong voice in wider marine planning initiatives.

The Shetland Shellfish Management Organisation (SSMO) operates under a unique and locally empowered framework to manage inshore shellfish fisheries around the Shetland Islands.
