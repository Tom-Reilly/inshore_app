Scientists on board research vessels conduct annual surveys to estimate Nephrops stock abundance around Scotland. At each survey station, a custom-built sledge is towed along the seabed and Nephrops burrows are counted and used to derive population density estimates.

Sampling of commercially trawl caught Nephrops is undertaken by fisheries observers onboard vessels. Co-sampling also takes place at some ports around Scotland, where commercial vessels bring ashore baskets of unwanted catch. Sampling at market of both trawl and creel caught Nephrops is carried out on a weekly basis at ports around Scotland. Nephrops are sexed and the carapace width measured using digital callipers. Care is taken when handling live Nephrops to avoid damaging the product.
