The Scottish Demersal Joint Observer Programme is a scientific project run by the Scottish Government’s Marine Directorate and the Scottish Fishermen’s Federation. The main purpose of the scheme is to collect biological data from fishing trips for stock assessments carried out by the International Council for the Exploration of the Sea.

How It Works
  • Observers cover ~1–2 % of trawl fishing trips each year.
  • They record biological details including species, size, and age.
  • Observers take samples of bycatch (fish and other species caught unintentionally) during sorting on board.

The information collected by the scientific observers is used in the statistical estimation of weights, numbers-at-length and numbers-at-age by species, area and fishery. These estimates are submitted to ICES for use in single species stock assessments. The bycatch weight estimates are also used in fisheries management.
