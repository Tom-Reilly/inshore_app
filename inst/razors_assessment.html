This is a trial fishery and assessment methodologies are still under development. Some of the razor clam trial areas are assessed regionally using a method called Length Cohort Analysis (LCA). This approach works well for data-limited fisheries and uses information from commercial landings, length frequency data (collected from trial particpants), and biological estimates like growth and natural mortality to estimate fishing mortality at length.

The assessments aim to understand whether reducing fishing effort (F) could lead to better long-term yields by allowing razor clams to grow larger before being caught. It is not possible to directly estimate the Maximum Sustainable Yield (MSY), instead scientists use a proxy, which estimates the fishing level that gives the best yield per recruit. If fishing exceeds this level, the stock is considered overexploited.

In addition to LCA, other tools like length-based indicators and LPUE trends help evaluate stock health.
