Razor clams (Ensis sp.) are marine bivalve molluscs commonly found around the coast of Europe, inhabiting sediments from fine sand to soft mud in the intertidal and sub-tidal areas. Often referred to as 'spoots', three species are widely distributed around Scotland; E.siliqua, E.magnus (also known as E.arcuatus) and E.ensis. E.siliqua (the 'pod' razor) is the razor clam species typically targeted by the fishery in Scotland.
